Real Estate Biotech Chicago

Welcome to the Intersection

Why I'm launching this blog to explore the fascinating convergence of real estate, biotech, and technology in Chicago

Brandon Nieves • December 20, 2024 • 5 min read

Most people think real estate and biotechnology operate in completely different worlds. I used to think so too. But after spending the last few years working as a commercial real estate broker while simultaneously serving on the board of ChiTownBio, Chicago's first community biotech lab, I've discovered something fascinating: these worlds aren't just connected—they're creating entirely new opportunities at their intersection.

Where It All Started

My journey into this intersection began somewhat accidentally. With a background in Computer Science and Economics from RIT, I initially thought I'd follow a traditional tech career path. After working as a product analyst at Amount and in various engineering roles, I found myself drawn to commercial real estate at Matanky Realty Group. The analytical thinking from my tech background turned out to be incredibly valuable—whether I was building financial models for property investments or optimizing our CRM systems.

But it was my involvement with ChiTownBio that really opened my eyes to the possibilities. As Treasurer and board member, I wasn't just managing finances—I was learning how labs actually function, how scientists think about space and equipment, and most importantly, how the physical environment can either enable or constrain innovation.

The Real Estate-Biotech Connection

Here's what most people don't realize: biotech companies have incredibly specific real estate needs that traditional commercial real estate often struggles to meet. We're not just talking about lab benches and fume hoods. These companies need specialized electrical systems, gas lines, waste management, vibration control, and biosafety infrastructure. The difference between a standard office space and a functional biotech facility can literally determine whether a startup succeeds or fails.

At ChiTownBio, I've seen firsthand how the right space can catalyze innovation. Our community lab isn't just about providing equipment—it's about creating an environment where biotech entrepreneurs can experiment, iterate, and build networks without the massive upfront costs of traditional lab space. It's a model that's replicating across cities, but the real estate component is often overlooked in discussions about innovation ecosystems.

Technology as the Bridge

My computer science background has become the bridge between these worlds. In real estate, I use data analysis to identify market trends and optimize investment strategies. In biotech, I help optimize lab operations and understand the technology infrastructure that modern life sciences companies need. But more importantly, I see how technology is reshaping both industries in ways that create new opportunities.

PropTech is revolutionizing how we analyze and manage real estate investments. BioTech is creating new categories of companies that need entirely new types of facilities. And at the intersection, we're seeing the emergence of innovation districts, incubators, and hybrid spaces that wouldn't have been possible—or profitable—just a decade ago.

Why Chicago Matters

Chicago is uniquely positioned to capitalize on this intersection. We have world-class research institutions, a strong industrial base, relatively affordable real estate (compared to the coasts), and a growing ecosystem of biotech and fintech companies. But we're still figuring out how to make all these pieces work together.

From Fulton Market's transformation into a tech hub to the emerging biotech clusters around the medical district, Chicago's innovation geography is evolving rapidly. Understanding these changes requires perspective from multiple angles—real estate fundamentals, technology trends, and the specific needs of emerging industries.

What's Coming Next

This blog is my attempt to document and analyze these intersections. I'll be exploring questions like: How do you evaluate the real estate potential of an emerging innovation district? What makes some biotech incubators successful while others struggle? How can traditional real estate investors understand and capitalize on the needs of tech and biotech companies?

I'll also share insights from my day-to-day work—the deals that work and the ones that don't, the lessons learned from building ChiTownBio, and the fascinating conversations that happen when you bring together real estate professionals, scientists, and technologists.

Join the Conversation

If you're working at any of these intersections—whether you're a real estate professional curious about emerging industries, a biotech entrepreneur navigating facility decisions, or a technologist interested in how physical space shapes innovation—I'd love to hear from you.

The most interesting opportunities often exist at the boundaries between established fields. That's where I'm focusing my attention, and I hope you'll join me for the journey.
